Texas Rig

The Texas Rig is a weedless setup ideal for fishing soft plastic baits through heavy cover.

Steps:

  1. Slide a bullet weight onto the line.
  2. Tie on an offset worm hook.
  3. Insert the hook into the head of the worm, rotate, and embed the hook point back into the body to make it weedless.

  • Category: Beginner

Drop Shot Rig

The Drop Shot Rig is perfect for presenting soft plastics just off the bottom with a natural, suspended action.

Steps:

  1. Tie a hook to the line with a Palomar knot, leaving a long tag end.
  2. Attach a weight to the end of the tag line.
  3. Nose-hook the soft plastic bait for maximum movement.

  • Category: Freshwater

Flapper Rig

The Flapper Rig is great for surf fishing, allowing natural bait to flutter freely to attract bottom feeders.

Steps:

  1. Tie a swivel to your mainline.
  2. Attach a short, strong leader to the swivel with a hook at the end.
  3. Clip the hook into a bait clip to streamline casting, then it releases on impact.

  • Category: Saltwater

Wacky Rig

The Wacky Rig is a simple, highly effective setup where the hook is placed through the middle of a soft plastic worm for a natural fall.

Steps:

  1. Tie a hook to your line.
  2. Insert the hook directly through the center of a soft plastic worm.
  3. Cast and let the bait flutter down with occasional twitches.

    Category: Beginner

Carolina Rig

The Carolina Rig is ideal for covering large areas of water, with the bait trailing behind a weight to stay near the bottom.

Steps:

  1. Slide a bullet weight and a bead onto your mainline.
  2. Tie on a swivel.
  3. Add a long leader (12-24 inches) with a hook and soft plastic bait.

  • Category: Freshwater

High Low Rig

The High Low Rig uses two hooks at different heights to target multiple species or increase hookup chances in bottom fishing.

Steps:

  1. Tie two dropper loops along the mainline.
  2. Attach hooks to each loop.
  3. Tie a weight at the bottom to keep the rig anchored.

  • Category: Saltwater
